================================================================================
  COST ALLOCATION — HOW THE INDUSTRY TRACKER PRICES MATERIALS AND JOBS
================================================================================

This document describes the full lifecycle of a material or finished product,
from the moment you acquire it to the moment a manufacturing profit figure
appears in the Industry Builder.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  PHASE 1 — ACQUIRING ITEMS (what creates cost records)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Two events create an acquisition cost record for an item:

  A) Market buy
     ─────────
     When ESI syncs your wallet, every buy transaction is read from
     CharacterWalletTransactionsModel (or the corp equivalent).

     backfill_wallet_buy_acquisitions() reads those transactions and writes a
     row to CharacterAssetHistoryModel:

         acquisition_source        = "wallet_transaction"
         acquisition_unit_cost     = unit_price you actually paid
         acquisition_total_cost    = unit_price × quantity
         acquisition_reference_id  = wallet transaction_id
         observed_at               = transaction date

     This is the authoritative buy price for that lot.

  B) Manufacturing job completion
     ─────────────────────────────
     When a job's status moves to "delivered" or "ready", the tracker resolves
     its cost snapshot via resolve_industry_job_cost_snapshot():

         1. If unit_build_cost is already persisted on the job row
            (stored when the job was last synced), use it directly.
         2. Otherwise, look up owned material purchase records (wallet
            transactions) and use their FIFO cost as the material basis,
            applying ME reduction and the actual ESI job fee.
         3. If no purchase records exist (e.g. the app was offline when the
            job started, or materials were bought before the wallet history
            window), fall back to estimating from SDE blueprint materials ×
            current ESI adjusted prices (build_cost_source =
            "market_snapshot_estimate").

     After all job snapshots are written, backfill_historical_market_costs()
     runs a second pass over every job that landed on market_snapshot_estimate
     with 0 % historical coverage and no per-material breakdown:

         For each such job:
           a. Determine the job's start_date (when materials were consumed).
           b. For each blueprint material, look up the Jita daily average
              price on or immediately before start_date from the
              market_history table (ESI /markets/{region}/history/).
              If the history is not yet cached, fetch it from ESI and store
              it for future use (market history goes back several years).
           c. Recalculate materials_cost, total_build_cost, and
              unit_build_cost using those date-specific prices.
           d. Write a per-material breakdown to historical_input_costs:
                { "type_id": { "unit_cost", "quantity",
                               "source": "historical_market_daily_average",
                               "reference_date": "YYYY-MM-DD" } }
           e. Set build_cost_source = "historical_market_price_on_start_date"
              and historical_material_coverage_fraction to the fraction of
              materials that could be priced from history (typically 1.0).

     This makes retrospective job costs significantly more accurate than
     using current ESI adjusted prices, which reflect today's market rather
     than the market on the day materials were consumed.

     The resolved unit_build_cost is then written to CharacterAssetHistoryModel:

         acquisition_source        = "industry_build"
         acquisition_unit_cost     = total_build_cost / output_quantity
         acquisition_reference_type = "industry_job"
         acquisition_reference_id  = job_id

     This means manufactured items carry their actual build cost, not a market
     price, as their acquisition cost.

  Note: sells are NOT acquisition events — they consume existing lots.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  PHASE 2 — BUILDING THE FIFO INVENTORY SNAPSHOT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

When the Industry Builder refreshes, _get_owned_item_inventory() runs once and
produces two maps that are shared across every product calculation:

    quantity_by_type_id        → how many units of each type you hold right now
    unit_cost_by_type_id       → the FIFO-weighted average unit cost per type

Steps:

  1. Query CharacterAssetsModel (+ corp) → build quantity_by_type_id
     (blueprints are excluded — they are handled separately).

  2. Query wallet transactions for those type_ids (character + corp).
     These are the raw buy/sell events ESI reported.

  3. Query completed industry jobs whose product_type_id is in the type set.
     Jobs become "buy" lots at their unit_build_cost.
     The cost source priority for each job is:
       a. historical_asset_acquisition_cost — actual material purchase records
          were matched from wallet transactions (most accurate).
       b. historical_market_price_on_start_date — no purchase records found,
          but Jita daily average prices on the job's start_date were used
          (see Phase 1B backfill). Accurate for jobs that ran before the
          wallet history window, provided ESI market history covers that date.
       c. market_snapshot_estimate — neither purchase records nor historical
          market data were available; current ESI adjusted prices were used
          (least accurate, may differ significantly from actual cost).

  4. Pass everything to build_fifo_remaining_lots_by_type():

         For each type_id:
           a. Sort all buy events (market + job) by date, oldest first.
           b. For each sell event, consume lots FIFO (oldest lot first).
           c. Align remaining lots to current on-hand quantity:
              - If lots total > on-hand: trim oldest excess (missing sells
                that ESI no longer reports are assumed to have consumed the
                oldest stock).
              - If lots total < on-hand: leave as-is (those extra units
                have no traceable cost basis).

         Result: a list of remaining FIFO lots per type_id, each carrying:
           { quantity, unit_price, acquisition_date, source }

  5. For each type, fifo_allocate_cost(lots, on_hand_qty) returns
     (total_cost, priced_qty). Divide to get the FIFO-weighted average:

         unit_cost = total_cost / priced_qty

  5b. Back-filling FIFO gaps caused by ESI's 30-day transaction cap:

         When FIFO lot reconstruction results in priced_qty < on_hand (some
         units have no traceable transaction history), the weighted average is
         extended using acquisition_unit_cost stored on the asset record:

             unpriced_units = on_hand - priced_qty
             backfill_unit  = weighted avg of asset.acquisition_unit_cost
             total_cost    += backfill_unit × unpriced_units
             priced_qty    += unpriced_units

         This means items bought before the ESI window still contribute their
         stored acquisition cost to the weighted average, rather than being
         silently dropped and forcing a fallback to CCP type averages.

  6. For types with no FIFO data at all (no transaction or job history):
     fall back through this chain:

         acquisition_unit_cost on the current asset record
           ↓ null or zero
         Most recent acquisition_unit_cost in CharacterAssetHistoryModel
           ↓ null or zero
         Current market sell price at the selected hub (Jita/Amarr/etc.)
           ↓ null or zero
         type_average_price (CCP market-wide average price)
           ↓ null or zero
         type_adjusted_price (CCP adjusted price used for job fees)
           ↓ null or zero
         ESI estimated item value (last resort)

     The market sell price step was added because CCP's type_average_price is a
     30-day cross-region trailing average that can be significantly off from
     the price you'd actually pay at your trade hub.

     Items that fall back to levels 3–5 are flagged as
     uses_unknown_owned_cost_basis = True (see Phase 4).


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  PHASE 3 — PLANNING A MANUFACTURING JOB (take-or-buy decision)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

For every product in the builder, _plan_take_or_buy_material_nodes() runs
against the SDE material list for that blueprint.

For each required material:

  owned_qty  = quantity_by_type_id.get(material_type_id, 0)
  required   = adjusted_quantity_after_ME_reduction

  IF owned_qty >= required:
      sourcing_strategy = "take"
      unit_price        = unit_cost_by_type_id[type_id]   ← FIFO-weighted avg
      price_source      = "owned_asset_item_value"
      take_quantity     = required
      buy_quantity      = 0

      If no cost is known (type not in unit_cost_by_type_id):
          unit_price    = market buy price
          uses_unknown_owned_cost_basis = True             ← warning flag

      Decrement available_owned_quantities[type_id] by required
      (prevents the same stock from being counted for two different products
       in the same refresh).

  IF 0 < owned_qty < required  (partial ownership):
      sourcing_strategy = "split"
      take_quantity     = owned_qty
      buy_quantity      = required - owned_qty
      unit_price        = blended average:
                          (fifo_price × take_qty + market_price × buy_qty) / required
      price_source      = "split_owned_and_market"

      available_owned_quantities[type_id] is set to 0.

      If no FIFO cost is known for the owned portion, market price is used for
      both halves and uses_unknown_owned_cost_basis = True.

  IF owned_qty == 0:
      sourcing_strategy = "buy"
      unit_price        = current market sell price
      price_source      = e.g. "market_sell_price_jita"
      take_quantity     = 0
      buy_quantity      = required


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  PHASE 4 — TOTAL JOB COST
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  material_cost  = Σ (unit_price × adjusted_quantity) for all materials
  job_fee        = facility_tax × Estimated Item Value of inputs
                   (EIV is CCP's adjusted_price × quantity, used for the
                    ESI-reported job cost)
  copy_cost      = amortised BPC copying cost (if applicable)
  invention_cost = expected invention material cost per run, adjusted for
                   probability (T2 items only).
                   Datacores and decryptors are priced at their FIFO-weighted
                   owned cost when available; market price is the fallback.

  total_cost     = material_cost + job_fee + copy_cost + invention_cost

  unit_build_cost = total_cost / output_quantity


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  PHASE 5 — SALE PROCEEDS AND PROFIT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  gross_revenue  = sell_price × output_quantity
  sales_tax      = gross_revenue × effective_sales_tax_rate
                   (base rate reduced by Accounting skill level)
  broker_fee     = gross_revenue × effective_broker_fee_rate
                   (base rate reduced by Broker Relations skill + standings)
  net_proceeds   = gross_revenue - sales_tax - broker_fee

  profit_amount           = net_proceeds - total_cost
  profit_margin_fraction  = profit_amount / net_proceeds
  isk_per_hour            = profit_amount / time_seconds × 3600


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  PHASE 6 — PRICING CONFIDENCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Three signals are combined into an overall confidence rating (High / Medium / Low):

  Market sell price confidence
    • How many sell orders exist at the hub?         (+/- score)
    • How old is the cached orderbook?               (+/- score)
    • Is regional volume active (7-day average)?     (+/- score)
    • Is hub liquidity high (days-of-supply metric)? (+/- score)

    Price anomaly risk feeds directly into this score by subtracting points:
      • Price vs unit material cost ratio
        > 1000×   → High risk  (large score penalty, strong manipulation signal)
        > 100×    → Medium risk
      • Price vs 7-day / 60-day historical average
        > 5×      → High risk
        > 2×      → Medium risk
        < 0.3×    → Medium risk (suspiciously cheap)
      • Thin orderbook (< 3 sell orders) → Low risk

    Anomaly risk is NOT a separate cap applied after the fact — it is baked into
    the market confidence score itself, which then flows into the overall rating.

  Material pricing confidence
    • Are all materials priced?
    • Do any materials have uses_unknown_owned_cost_basis = True?
      If so → reason text is added, and the "all have known cost basis" condition
      fails, preventing the material confidence from reaching High. However, the
      resulting level may be Medium OR Low depending on the fraction of priced
      materials (≥ 80% priced → Medium, otherwise → Low). There is no hard cap
      to Medium — unknown cost basis only blocks High.

  Overall = min(market_confidence, material_confidence)
  (anomaly risk is already reflected in market_confidence via score penalties)

  The full list of reasons behind the score is shown in the drilldown panel
  when you click a row in the Industry Builder.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  COST SOURCE LABELS (visible in the material drilldown table)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Job-level build_cost_source values (stored on industry job rows):

  "historical_asset_acquisition_cost"         All materials matched from wallet
                                              purchase records (FIFO). Most accurate.
  "historical_market_price_on_start_date"     No purchase records; Jita daily avg
                                              prices on job start_date used instead.
                                              Per-material breakdown stored in
                                              historical_input_costs.
  "market_snapshot_estimate"                  No purchase records and no ESI history
                                              available; current ESI adjusted prices
                                              used. Least accurate.
  "mixed_historical_and_market_estimate"      Partial purchase record coverage;
                                              remainder estimated from market prices.

  The above may be suffixed with "+actual_invention_job" or
  "+expected_invention_material_cost" for T2 jobs.

  Per-material source values (inside historical_input_costs JSON):

  "historical_asset_acquisition_cost"         Actual purchase lot from wallet
  "historical_market_daily_average"           Jita daily avg on job start_date
                                              (set by the Phase 1B backfill)

  Material-level cost source labels (visible in the drilldown table):

  "owned_asset_item_value"            FIFO-weighted average of what you paid
  "owned_asset_acquisition_cost"      Single acquisition_unit_cost from asset record
  "split_owned_and_market"            Blended: take_qty at FIFO avg + buy_qty at market
  "market_sell_price_<hub>"           Current best-N sell orders at hub
  "asset_average_price"               CCP market-wide average (type_average_price)
  "asset_adjusted_price"              CCP adjusted price (used for job fee EIV)
  "market_snapshot_estimate"          Estimated from SDE + current adjusted prices

  "Est. Cost?" column in the drilldown: ⚠ est.
  Means the material was taken from inventory but no purchase or build record
  could be found — market price was used as a substitute.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  IMPORTANT LIMITATIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  1. ESI wallet transaction history is capped at ~30 days.
     Items bought earlier than that have no wallet transaction lots. The FIFO
     gap is mitigated by:
       - Back-filling unpriced units from acquisition_unit_cost on the asset
         record (Phase 2, step 5b).
       - For industry jobs: using the Phase 1B backfill to price materials
         from Jita daily average prices on the job's start_date rather than
         current ESI adjusted prices. ESI market history goes back several
         years, so this works even for jobs that completed long before the
         tracker was installed (build_cost_source =
         "historical_market_price_on_start_date").
       - For jobs where even market history is unavailable: estimating from
         current ESI adjusted prices (build_cost_source =
         "market_snapshot_estimate"). These estimates can be significantly
         wrong if market prices have moved since the job ran.
     Units with none of the above still fall back to market sell price, then
     to CCP type_average_price as a last resort.

  2. Unit cost is a FIFO-weighted average across the whole on-hand stock,
     not a per-lot assignment to a specific job.
     Two jobs planned in the same refresh both see the same per-unit average,
     not "job A gets your cheapest lot, job B gets the more expensive one".

  3. The inventory snapshot is taken once at refresh time and is not updated
     when a job is submitted.  Re-run the refresh after starting new jobs to
     see updated availability.
